Wharton Data Science

My experience making semifinals!

This year, the Wharton Data Science competition was about NHL teams and computing scores to predict which teams are most likely to win and therefore how to make a good bracket. I knew very little about hockey going into this, so it was absolutely a fun learning experience on that front.

This was my first major data science competition, so I am very much happy with my teams's placement! There were a lot of very cool piece of analysis we had to do, primarily based around adjusting from the ideal world that statistics exist in to real world world. This meant cleanup and projections over 60 minutes of game time rather than the actual data we had, which was likely an analysis that made our results stand out.

The semi-final round was about making a presentation, and this was difficult because we had to condense a lot of complex analysis into just a few slides. The challenge was that there's a difference between simply submitting a pptx file and actually giving the presentation: typically, I like to have very little information on the slides and talk a lot, but that wasn't an option. We added a lot of information to the slides, and that may have been what ultimately lost us the qualification.

Overall, I thought it was a very fun experience, and I am excited to take part in it again next year!
